Project Description

Proper illumination in the mouth is crucial in dentistry, but current lighting options present significant drawbacks. Overhead lamps cast shadows, requiring disruptive manual adjustments. Headlamps offer shadow-free focus but are heavy, causing ergonomic issues and neck strain. Our project will develop an automated overhead dental light system, combining headlamp precision with overhead comfort to eliminate minor adjustments and boost efficiency.

Favourite Capstone Memory

One of our favourite memories was the sense of momentum that built up during our final integration week. We spent countless hours in our second home, the SYDE workshop, with each of us testing, adjusting, and celebrating small breakthroughs that pushed the system closer to working end‑to‑end. Visiting our stakeholder in Burlington was another highlight because it grounded our work in a real clinical workflow and reminded us why the project mattered. We also had fun with the creative side of symposium prep, from making a jingle to hand-printing merch and baking cookies for our booth.

Biggest Challenges

Balancing budget constraints with performance needs was a constant challenge, especially when lower cost components demanded hours of debugging while higher quality parts stretched our limits. Those trade-offs became even more apparent during full system integration, where optics, mechanics, electronics, and AI all had to work seamlessly together. You only start to see the weakest links once everything is integrated, and with tight constraints those issues become much harder to fix. The experience pushed us to think holistically and make careful decisions at every stage.

Takeaways

Our biggest takeaway is how grounding it is to see your project through a real user's eyes. Our visit to Burlington to a real dental clinic completely reframed our engineering goals by showing us which features actually mattered in a clinical workflow and which ones were unnecessary complexity. It taught us that good engineering is not about adding more capability, but about simplifying until the system does exactly what the user needs, reliably and intuitively.

Advice for Future BME Students

Talk to mentors and technical staff early because their experience can prevent you from wasting time on avoidable mistakes. Just as importantly, choose teammates who communicate clearly and share similar expectations for workload and timelines (having the same sense of humour is also a plus!). A strong team dynamic makes the entire process more enjoyable and helps you navigate challenges with confidence.


What's Next?

We plan to refine the prototype into a polished portfolio piece that showcases our interdisciplinary engineering skills. The concept also has potential for real clinical use, especially in dental environments where precise, automated lighting can improve workflow. There is room for future teams or collaborators to build on our foundation and explore more advanced control strategies or improved hardware.
