Project Description

Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) is an early indicator of dementia, yet traditional diagnostic methods are often inaccurate, expensive, and require clinical administration. Cognify was developed as an accessible, at-home cognitive monitoring tool for individuals under 65, integrating language, memory, and eye-tracking assessments through interactive digital tasks. By analyzing trends over time, Cognify provides users and healthcare providers with objective insights, enabling early health intervention. This approach bridges the gap between clinical assessments and everyday cognitive health monitoring, making early detection more practical, personalized, and proactive.

Favourite Capstone Memory

Our favourite capstone memory was initially sitting down with our stakeholders to really talk through our problem space. In that moment it reaffirmed to us that there was a clear gap in detecting early cognitive decline

Biggest Challenges

Our biggest challenge was integrating multiple subsystems (eye tracking, speech analysis, and memory testing) into the application while also navigating data privacy and storage concerns, given that we collected personal health-related metrics.

Takeaways

We realized that strong communication can make or break a project, and that things can fall apart fast if the team isn’t aligned on goals, priorities, and expectations

Advice for Future BME Students

Don't be afraid to fail fast, listen closely to your stakeholders, and design around their needs (not your assumptions).
