Project Description

Antibiotic resistance (ABR) is a growing global threat, yet identifying resistant infections such as urinary tract infections still depends on slow, lab-based testing. In under-resourced settings, harsh environmental conditions and limited infrastructure make these tests impractical, leading to delayed or ineffective treatment. ABRDetect addresses this gap with a compact, low-cost screening device that uses an electromagnetic biosensor to rapidly detect resistance-associated enzymatic activity through resonance frequency shifts. This enables point-of-care screening in settings such as refugee camps, supporting earlier treatment decisions and better patient outcomes.

Overall Capstone Experience

Capstone Described in 3 Words:

Versatile, Innovation, Troubleshooting

Favourite Capstone Memory

Our favorite capstone memory was the moment our temperature control system finally worked. After spending hours over two weeks soldering and assembling it, and running into multiple setbacks along the way, seeing it function successfully was incredibly rewarding. It made all the effort feel completely worth it.

Biggest Challenges

  1. It was very tedious working with bacteria and enzymes. We often spent more than six hours a day continuously standing in the lab, constantly stressed about not making any mistakes. During testing, we had to ensure our process was consistent each time and that we were not introducing any sources of error. Many times, we would spend several hours conducting experiments only to get poor results. This was discouraging and affected our motivation, but we always tried our best to understand the root causes and stay motivated to restart.
  2. Since we soldered all of our electronic components onto a perfboard, careful planning was essential to avoid issues after assembly. We had to be extremely precise and cautious during soldering, while also learning and adapting along the way (for example, using screw terminals when certain components could not be soldered directly). Safety was also an important consideration. Although we made some mistakes throughout the process, these ultimately helped us refine and improve our system.
  3. When printing the CAD model, we went through several iterations to get the design right. Ensuring proper attachment types and tolerances was critical, especially since our parts were relatively large and we wanted to minimize material waste and cost. We also consulted others and ran simulations to verify proper temperature flow. Overall, this process required significant time, iteration, and attention to detail.

Takeaways

Our biggest takeaway from Capstone is the importance of maintaining a positive and resilient mindset throughout the engineering process. We learned that challenges and setbacks are inevitable, but success comes from focusing on solutions rather than problems: taking a step back, identifying root causes, using available resources, and applying engineering judgment to move forward. Ultimately, engineering is not about achieving perfection on the first try, but about continuously iterating, improving, and refining to reach a better solution.

Advice for Future BME Students

Just like any other phase of life, university will have its ups and downs. When things don’t work out, it’s important to take a step back and remind yourself why you are here and what your purpose in BME is. Surround yourself with positive influences, maintain healthy lifestyle habits, and make full use of the resources available to you. At the end of the day, staying grounded and resilient will help you navigate challenges and make the most of your experience.
