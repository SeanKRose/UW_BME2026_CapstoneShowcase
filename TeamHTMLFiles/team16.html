Project Description

Neuroendovascular surgery, a minimally-invasive surgery technique targeting brain diseases like cancer and aneurysms, has revolutionized how we treat brain diseases in adults, but access for Canadian children is limited. Pediatric cases require specialized training due to their complex geometry and unique diseases, which is currently scarce across Canada. VascuLearn, in collaboration with SickKids, is bridging this gap with a pediatric neurovascular simulator. By using a custom pump to mimic blood flow through 3D-printed vessel models based on patient MRI scans, the simulator provides affordable, high-fidelity training, ensuring children can access this gold-standard care.



Qualification for Specializations & Options

This project fulfills the capstone requirements of the Mechatronics option for Biomedical Engineering and the Biomechanics option for Mechatronics Engineering.

Favourite Capstone Memory

Biggest Challenges

  1. Understanding complex fluid dynamics and how they relate in a physical prototype beyond theory.
  2. Balancing scope and ambition for the project, it is extremely easy to dream big and run out of time with the open-ended nature of capstone.
  3. Individual knowledge siloing that results in one expert for each component and when that expert gets stuck.

Takeaways

Understanding the full cycle of an engineering project from stakeholder engagement for finding a problem space, to validation of a prototype. Along with working through the project with a team.

Advice for Future BME Students

Engage in many unique stakeholders as possible using 362 to fully define a problem space you are interested in and from which you can see a strong project coming from. Don’t be afraid to pivot early on and choose your team wisely. Also, apply for funding early and often - too many times funding is left on the table because teams don’t apply!
